Asynchronous Communication

Introduction

Why Asynchronous Communication?

Now that you understand how to communicate in real-time, let’s discuss why (and how) you might want to participate asynchronously.

There are plenty of team communication opportunities that don’t require everyone to participate. You wouldn’t call a meeting just to ask questions about the format or due date of the paper, right? But it’s important that your team has a single place to exchange these types of messages so that the entire team can benefit from them. Having a message history allows the team to review what was said, which is great for documenting your project.

Another benefit of this is that you don’t have to wait for a time when the entire team is available for a meeting. Members who were not able to participate can skim through the conversation and provide more input later, if necessary.
