Real-Time Communication

Choosing a tool for the team

You might immediately think that a phone call is the best way to handle group communication. A "conference call" is the default image many of us have when we think about a remote meeting. But those types of calls work best when the communication is one-way – as in, everyone in the call needs to hear the same information simultaneously. Anyone who has been on a contentious conference call can tell you that they can get messy, quickly.

Video conferences are rapidly becoming the norm for many virtual collaborations. They allow users to see each other (body language is important in communication!!), and it provides more of a sense of "being there with one another. Most video conference software also allows for screen and/or document sharing, so it's easy to show articles or slides when the conversation demands. Some tools also include virtual collaboration boards – whiteboards or Kanban boards – that allow the team to sketch out ideas quickly while working together in real time.

We'll review three potential tools for real-time communication. You're free to choose another if you'd like, but you must ensure that it's accessible to the entire team.

  • Zoom
  • Microsoft Teams Meetings
  • Google Hangouts
