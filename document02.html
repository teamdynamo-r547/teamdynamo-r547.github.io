Document Collaboration

Choosing a tool for the team

You probably have a computer where you tend to store your work for your courses. You might use a service to back up your documents to the cloud in case something happens to your computer. But when you are working on shared documents with multiple individuals, you must ensure that you’re doing so in a way that the entire team can collaborate on demand.

The good news is that there are lots of options available for you that will help you do this. All of these platforms will work for both your own documents as well as those for this (and other) team projects. Gone are the days of emailing documents back and forth – they can easily get out-of-sync and changes can quickly get lost. Working from a centralized document repository will keep your team on track for success.

We’ll review three potential tools for document collaboration. You’re free to choose another if you’d like, but you must ensure that it’s accessible to the entire team.

  • Google Drive
  • Microsoft Teams
  • Microsoft OneDrive
