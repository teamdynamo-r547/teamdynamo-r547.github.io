Asynchronous Communication

Choosing a tool for the team

When choosing a tool to exchange messages with your team, the first question you should ask yourself is: How do you exchange messages with people now? Then consider why you have chosen that platform. Most likely, you use different tools for different individuals or groups. Why is that?

The answer is: there is not “one” correct solution. You might text with your parents but use DMs with your friends. Perhaps you have an internship somewhere that uses a messaging platform in the business. If so, that the platform was decided for you – without your input.

You’ll need to consider your teammates, their technology, and the capabilities of the messaging systems that are available. The team may not choose your preferred option, but it’s still important for you to use that tool when working with the group to maintain continuity and keep the entire team on the same page.

We’ll review four potential tools for asynchronous communication. You’re free to choose another if you’d like, but you must ensure that it’s accessible to the entire team.

  • Slack
  • Microsoft Teams
  • WhatsApp
  • Group SMS
