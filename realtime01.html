Real-Time Communication

Introduction

Why Real-Time Communication?

Real-time communication is important when big decisions need to be made quickly. You’ll find that these meetings are most useful toward the start and end of your project – when your team first gets on the same page and distributes the workload, and when everything starts to come together. But you’ll also want to ensure that you have regular real-time meetings to show progress, compare your work, and generally ensure that the team is on the right track.

Think about putting together an in-person meeting. You must choose a location that is convenient for everyone – and one that everyone can access. You couldn’t have a team meeting at a bar if some of your teammates are under 21, for example. The same is true for virtual meetings. You must ensure that the meeting space you’ve chosen is convenient and available to everyone. This means you must have a good understanding of the technical specifications for different communication platforms and the technical capabilities (or limitations) of your team.

The benefit to collaborating online is that geography isn’t much of a factor – everyone can join from wherever they are. But you must still be aware of scheduling issues (if some team members are in different time zones, for example) and work with your teammates to ensure the meetings are convenient for all.
